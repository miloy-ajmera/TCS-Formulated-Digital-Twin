CI 74160, also called copper phthalocyanine (CuPc) is a bright, crystalline, synthetic, blue pigment which is used as a colorant in cosmetics. It is permitted for use in all cosmetics as per the European Commission directive and in China, however, it is not allowed in cosmetics as per the US FDA. Its chemical formula is C32H16CuN8.

What is CI 74160 used for?

CI 74160 is used as a cosmetic colorant in several applications:

Toothpastes - Excipients, and humectants are very useful ingredients to allow a better behavior and fluidity of a dental paste. Various flavors and colors form the excipients which enhance the product’s marketing. CI 74160 acts as a blue dye for toothpaste
Soaps: Since it is oil soluble, it is a coloring agent for soaps
Makeup products: Nail polishes, powders, sticks




Origin

It can be obtained via two chemical processes:

The phthalonitrile process, in which phthalonitrile is condensed in the presence of cuprous chloride at 180-200°C
The phthalic anhydride/urea process, in which condensation of phthalic anhydride with urea, in presence of copper ions, with or without added chlorophthalic anhydride; and subsequent conversion to alpha phase and stabilization is carried out
